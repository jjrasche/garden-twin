# Stowell's Evergreen Corn - Research Sources

## Historical Background

**HIGH CONFIDENCE**

Stowell's Evergreen is one of the oldest sweet corn varieties still in production, predating 1849. It remains a favorite for its old-fashioned sweet flavor and reliability.

**Distinguishing characteristic:** Kernels hold in the milk stage on the stalk longer than most sweet corn, giving an extended harvest window without turning starchy.

**Sources:**
- [Baker Creek](https://www.rareseeds.com/corn-stowell-s-evergreen-sweet)
- [Thresh Seed Co](https://www.threshseed.com/products/stowells-evergreen)

## Spacing Requirements

**HIGH CONFIDENCE**

**Used:** 0.48 plants/sq ft

**Calculation:**
- In-row spacing: 8-12 inches (most sources recommend 8-12")
- Between-row spacing: 24-36 inches (most sources say 30-36")
- Area per plant: 10" × 30" = 300 sq in = 2.08 sq ft
- **Plants per sq ft: 1 / 2.08 = 0.48**

**Sources:**
- [Bucktown Seed](https://bucktownseed.com/products/stowells-evergreen-sweet-corn) - "8-12 inches apart"
- [Smart Gardener](https://www.smartgardener.com/plants/1515-corn-stowells-evergreen/overview) - "6-12 inches apart"
- [Territorial Seed](https://territorialseed.com/products/stowells-evergreen) - "2-3 feet apart" (between rows)

**Note:** Plant in blocks (not single rows) for proper wind pollination

## Yield Per Plant

**MEDIUM CONFIDENCE**

**Calculation:**

## Nutrition Data

**HIGH CONFIDENCE**

**Source:** USDA FoodData Central - Sweet Corn, yellow, raw

**Note:** Stowell's Evergreen has white kernels, but nutritional content is similar to yellow sweet corn. White and yellow sweet corn differ primarily in carotenoid content (vitamin A), with white corn having slightly less.

**Per pound (454g) of fresh sweet corn:**
- Calories: 390
- Protein: 15g
- Carbs: 85g
- Fat: 6.4g
- Fiber: 9.1g
- Vitamins/minerals: standard sweet corn values
