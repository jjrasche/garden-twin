## Yield Per Plant

**MEDIUM CONFIDENCE**

### Ear Weight Calculation
- [USDA via Weigh School](https://weighschool.com/sweet-corn-weights-servings/) - Medium ear: 102g (0.225 lbs)
- [USDA via FatSecret](https://foods.fatsecret.com/calories-nutrition/usda/yellow-sweet-corn?portionid=34105&portionamount=1.000) - Medium ear (6.75-7.5"): ~3.6 oz (0.225 lbs)
- [Nutritionix](https://www.nutritionix.com/i/usda/corn-1-ear-medium-6-0.75-to-7-1-2-long-yields/513fceb575b8dbbc21001520) - Medium ear: 102g

**Calculation:**
- Golden Bantam ears: 6-7" (small-medium)
- Estimated weight per ear: 0.2 lbs (conservative, smaller than standard medium ear)
- Ears per plant: 1.5 (average of 1-2 ears)
- **Total yield: 1.5 × 0.2 = 0.3 lbs per plant**

**Note:** This is lower than Wapsie Valley (0.25 lbs per plant) because Wapsie Valley is a dent corn measured as dried weight, while Golden Bantam is fresh sweet corn measured as fresh weight. However, the architect may want to verify this is the correct comparison.

## Spacing Requirements

**HIGH CONFIDENCE**

- [Fine Seeds Growing Guide](https://www.thefineseeds.com/blogs/news/golden-bantam-corn-growing-guide) - "Plant 12" apart in rows, rows 30-36 inches apart"
- [True Leaf Market](https://trueleafmarket.com/products/corn-op-golden-bantam) - "Plant 8-12 inches apart"
- [UF Seeds](https://www.ufseeds.com/product/early-golden-bantam-corn-seed/COEG.html) - "8-12 inches apart, rows 30-36 inches"

**Calculation:**
- In-row spacing: 12"
- Between-row spacing: 30" (conservative)
- Area per plant: 12" × 30" = 360 sq in = 2.5 sq ft
- **Plants per sq ft: 1 / 2.5 = 0.4**

**Note:** This is wider spacing than Wapsie Valley (0.67 plants/sq ft). Golden Bantam is an older heirloom that may need more space, or sources may be conservative. Could tighten to 0.5-0.6 plants/sq ft for intensive gardening.

## Harvest Window

**MEDIUM CONFIDENCE**

- [Oklahoma State Extension](https://extension.okstate.edu/fact-sheets/sweet-corn-production.html) - "Each planting mature for 7-10 days"
- [Old Farmer's Almanac](https://www.almanac.com/plant/corn) - "Harvest every 2 weeks for successive plantings"
- [MIgardener](https://migardener.com/blogs/blog/when-to-harvest-corn) - "Peak quality for 2-3 days, extended harvest 7-10 days"

**Used:** 10 days (upper end of range for home garden successive picking)

**Note:** Peak eating quality is 2-3 days, but ears remain harvestable for 7-10 days. This is the "harvest window" for practical home garden use.

## Historical Context

Golden Bantam was introduced by **W. Atlee Burpee in 1902** and is credited with popularizing yellow sweet corn in America (previously, yellow corn was considered livestock feed only).
