Reading the map

X axis = along the 42" tray row (4 trays x 10.5"). Y axis = across 20" tray width. Two 24" bars are butted end-to-end, centered over the trays. The junction at x=24" creates a slight hotspot where both bars contribute ~59% each.

Sensitivity: drag the baseline slider

The map geometry (contour shapes) is fixed physics. The absolute PPFD numbers scale linearly with the baseline. Drag "Baseline PPFD @ 4" between 448 (-20%) and 672 (+20%) to see how measurement error affects crop zones. The Photone app measurement replaces this slider with a real number.

If the junction zone exceeds 300 PPFD (turns red), raise the height slider until it drops below 300.
